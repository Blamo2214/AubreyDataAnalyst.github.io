About Me

Analytical depth with a reporter's instinct for the story

I am a data analyst and independent researcher with a particular interest in financial markets, electoral behavior, public policy, and the economic forces that shape everyday life. My goal is to build a career in market or political analysis where I can combine technical research with clear, evidence-based communication.

My interest in analytics began before I had formal training. As a teenager, I studied commodity prices, interest rates, options behavior, and macroeconomic trends. I taught myself to organize and analyze historical data in Excel and eventually built Monte Carlo simulations to explore option-contract outcomes. That curiosity developed into more than a decade of hands-on experience following and trading crude oil, interest-rate, equity-index, and volatility products.

I later earned a Bachelor of Science in Data Analytics from Southern New Hampshire University and am continuing my education through graduate study in data analytics. Across academic, freelance, and personal projects, I have worked with SQL, Python, R, Excel, Tableau, Power BI, relational databases, predictive models, and statistical visualization.

What separates my work from purely technical analysis is that I care just as much about context as calculation. I want to know why a pattern exists, what assumptions produced it, who is affected, and how the finding should change a decision. Whether I am analyzing volatility regimes, voter coalitions, enterprise data architecture, or operating costs in my own business, I focus on translating evidence into a useful conclusion.
